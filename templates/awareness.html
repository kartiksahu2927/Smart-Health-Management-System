🦠 Cholera

A severe diarrheal disease caused by contaminated food or water.

Symptoms

  • Sudden watery diarrhea
  • Severe dehydration
  • Vomiting
  • Rapid heart rate
🌡️ Typhoid

Caused by bacteria in contaminated water or food.

Symptoms

  • High fever (up to 40°C)
  • Headache and body aches
  • Weakness, abdominal pain
💧 Diarrhea

Most common in areas with poor sanitation.

Symptoms

  • Loose, watery stools
  • Abdominal cramping
  • Dehydration signs
🦠 Hepatitis A

Viral infection affecting the liver, spread through contaminated water.

Symptoms

  • Jaundice
  • Dark urine
  • Fatigue and weakness

🛡️ Prevention Guidelines

✓ DO's

  • Boil water before drinking
  • Wash hands with soap frequently
  • Keep food covered
  • Get vaccinated where available
  • Use clean toilet facilities

✗ DON'Ts

  • Don't drink untreated water
  • Don't eat undercooked food
  • Don't practice open defecation
  • Don't ignore symptoms
  • Don't store water too long

💧 Safe Water Practices

Boiling: Heat water to 100°C for at least 1 minute to kill pathogens.
Chlorination: Add 2-3 drops of food-grade bleach per liter, wait 30 minutes.
Storage: Store water in clean, covered containers; don't keep more than 24 hours untreated.
Testing: pH should be 6.5-8.5; chlorine residual should be 0.5-1.0 mg/L.
